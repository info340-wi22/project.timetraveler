Because of the outbreak of COVID-19, people all over the world have to stay at home to work and study. Remote/hybrid work is continuing and trending permanently (Lydia Saad and Ben Wigert, 2022). That means people located in different cities or countries gather together online. Our group finds that it is very time-consuming and error-prone to convert the time difference, which makes things, such as scheduling meeting time and figuring out deadlines of work, harder.

Converting time zones and scheduling events automatically by writing a web application is tangible for solving the “planning hard” problem. We are interested in making a time zone converter with outstanding user experience, which can help the users to make time schedules and to-do lists easily and tangible.

There are a lot of time converters on the internet. Take timeanddate.com as an instance. It is actually a very good example of alleviating the pain of converting time among locations that the users are interested in. It also has a feature of recognizing effective work time. When the location is during the daytime except for holidays, there is a green dot beside the location suggesting that the location is an effective work time. But things get a little bit more complex when the user wants to look at several periods of time at the same time. It is also hard to keep track of the event list on this website.
